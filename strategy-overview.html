Our Story · Why We Must Change

5 Years of Growth Built on Fragile Foundations

Understanding how we got here is essential to understanding where we must go. This is the honest account of our evolution, the complexity we created, and the turning point we now stand at.

The Evolution Timeline
Phase 1 · 2021–2022 · Growth & Fragmentation
We grew fast — and grew apart
Medicaid expansion, new state contracts, and regulatory mandates drove rapid technology investment. Systems grew organically, market-by-market, to meet urgent needs. QNXT became the core platform but was surrounded by a growing ecosystem of point solutions and market-specific applications.
✓ Fast market growth enabled ✗ Fragmented architecture emerged ✗ Duplicate capabilities across markets
Phase 2 · 2023–2024 · Scale Without Integration
Capability breadth grew — but complexity exploded
Continued expansion and retention focus drove more enhancements layered onto existing systems. Integration overhead increased as claims, enrollment, clinical, and provider platforms needed to talk to each other — but were never designed to. Early digital and experience investments began, but on top of an already fragmented base.
✓ Capability breadth increased ✗ Complexity exploded ✗ Technical debt accelerated
Phase 3 · 2024–2025 · Complexity Peaks
High cost, low agility, and leadership transition
Significant business and technology leadership changes compounded the challenge. Teams experienced frequent shifts in direction and priorities. The portfolio ballooned to ~350 applications with overlapping, redundant capabilities. Run-the-business spend crowded out innovation. Systems became tightly coupled and harder to change. Cost structure deteriorated without a clear line of sight to ROI.
✗ ~350 apps, high maintenance cost ✗ Low delivery agility ✗ Limited ROI transparency ✗ Inconsistent ownership & decision rights
Phase 4 · Now · Turning Point
Leadership alignment. A clear direction. The moment to move.
With Katerina Guerraz, Gregory Krause, and Jeffrey Krasner aligned on direction, we are shifting from building for Medicaid individually to leveraging Aetna enterprise capabilities for Medicaid advantage. Strategic platforms — Aetna Health, OneCM, QNXT modernization, GCP data — are now the path forward.
✓ Leadership aligned on direction ✓ Product operating model launching ✓ Enterprise platform migration underway
"Over the past 5 years, Medicaid Technology grew rapidly to meet market and regulatory demand — but did so through fragmented systems, evolving leadership, and project-based execution. This enabled growth — but created complexity, cost, and inefficiency. Today marks a turning point. We are shifting to a unified, product-driven, platform-based model designed to reduce cost, improve agility, and deliver measurable outcomes for members and the business."
— Aetna Medicaid Technology Leadership · 2026 Strategic Direction
The 5 Root Causes We Are Solving For
1. Fragmented Ecosystem
~350 applications with duplicate capabilities driving maintenance cost. Solution: platform consolidation, app rationalization, shared enterprise services replacing point solutions.
2. Run-the-Business Dominance
Large investments locked in stability and compliance, crowding out innovation. Solution: cloud-native infrastructure to reduce run cost; free investment capacity for product advancement.
3. Technical Debt & Legacy Architecture
Years of layered enhancements on legacy systems. Integration-heavy, brittle architecture. Solution: ATLAS remedy replacement, QNXT modernization, Azure cloud migration, API-first design.
4. Lack of Unified Operating Model
Project-based funding without product ownership. Opaque intake and prioritization. Solution: product operating model with OKRs, outcome-based funding, value streams, clear decision rights.
5. Leadership & Org Instability (Historical)
Frequent shifts in direction slowed standardization. Inconsistent decision rights. Solution: aligned leadership team under Greg Krause, clear product ownership, stable strategic direction for 3-year horizon.
The Vision: From Cost Center → Value Engine
Projects → Products. Fragmentation → Platforms. Manual → Automated. On-prem → Cloud. Reactive → Proactive. A unified, product-based, cloud-native Medicaid platform delivering measurable outcomes by 2029.
