2021 – 2022
Growth at all costs
+12
states serving Medicaid members
Every market built its own stack — fast, but separately.
New state contracts and regulatory mandates drove urgency. Teams moved quickly and built what worked — market by market. The instinct was right. The outcome was duplication: separate claims systems, separate enrollment platforms, separate portals — for each market. The foundation fractures here.
✓ 12+ states served ✓ Contracts won ✗ No shared design ✗ Siloed by market
Root cause planted
Fragmented ecosystem begins
The same capability built 3× across markets. ~350 apps begin accumulating. No shared platform.
No unified operating model
Project-based funding with no shared product ownership. Teams optimize for their market, not the enterprise.
2
2023 – 2024
Complexity compounds
40%
of changes touch 3+ systems
More capability, but now everything is connected — badly.
Expanding markets demanded more features across claims, enrollment, clinical, and provider platforms. The instinct was to integrate what existed. The result was a web of point-to-point connections that nobody fully understood. Every change needed to be tested in 6 places. Run-the-business spend crowded out innovation.
✓ Capability breadth grew ✓ Digital work began ✗ Integration debt exploded ✗ Change became expensive
Root causes compounding
Technical debt accelerates
Legacy systems enhanced rather than replaced. Brittle integrations mean every release is a risk. Velocity drops year over year.
Run-the-business dominates
83% of investment consumed by stability, resiliency, and compliance. Only 17 cents of every dollar can go toward what members actually need.
3
2024 – 2025
Complexity peaks
350
applications in the portfolio
High cost, low agility, and leadership in transition.
Run cost reached $105M annually. The portfolio ballooned to ~350 applications. With every change touching dozens of systems, delivery slowed. Leadership transitions reset priorities mid-stream. Nobody had a clear view of what was delivering value versus what was just running.
✗ $105M annual run cost ✗ ~350 apps, unclear ownership ✗ Slow delivery cycles ✗ Limited ROI visibility
Root causes at peak
Cost structure out of control
Application sprawl and legacy maintenance alone consume ~$105M/year. The system is expensive to run and expensive to change.
Leadership instability
Frequent leadership transitions reset strategy mid-stream. Inconsistent decision rights mean the same conversations happen twice. Progress stalls.

Over the past 5 years, Medicaid Technology grew rapidly to meet market and regulatory demand — but did so through fragmented systems, evolving leadership, and project-based execution. This enabled growth — but created complexity, cost, and inefficiency. Today marks a turning point. We are shifting to a unified, product-driven, platform-based model designed to reduce cost, improve agility, and deliver measurable outcomes for members and the business.
